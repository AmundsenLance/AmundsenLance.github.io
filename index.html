Benchmarks

pvpp-agent-decision-layer-demo

This demonstration compares traditional scalar-scoring AI agents with a PV-PP Agent Decision Layer under enterprise incident conditions. It shows how agents that optimize a single score can choose locally attractive actions that damage compliance, recovery capacity, or security, while PV-PP focuses on preserving governing thresholds and viable recovery corridors.

Grenade Self-Sacrifice Benchmark

The PV-PP Grenade Self-Sacrifice Benchmark is a test environment used to compare how different decision systems respond in an extreme life-or-death scenario. An agent must choose between self-preservation and sacrificing itself to save others from a grenade blast. It is designed to measure whether a system can recognize and justify costly prosocial action, not just maximize narrow self-interest.

AI Gridworld Safe Benchmark

The AI Grid World Safe Benchmark is a test environment used to compare how different AI systems make decisions in a simple grid-based world where success is not enough on its own. Agents must reach goals while avoiding hazards, bad shortcuts, unsafe actions, and traps. It is designed to measure whether an AI can pursue objectives safely, not just efficiently.

Research Papers

Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) - Why It Works

Alcoholics Anonymous - Why It Works paper examines AA as a practical recovery system by focusing on the concrete mechanisms that help people stop drinking and rebuild stable lives. It explains how meetings, sponsorship, social structure, identity change, repeated accountability, and meaning frameworks can produce durable recovery outcomes beyond debates over ideology.
